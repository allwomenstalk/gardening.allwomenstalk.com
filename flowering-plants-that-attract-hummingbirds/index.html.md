# Beautiful Flowering Plants That Attract Hummingbirds ...

Every garden needs some **flowering plants that attract hummingbirds**. Nature has a perfect flying machine. They are small, fast, and can even fly upside down. There are over 300 distinct types of the species. They hum but they do not know the words! Welcome to the world of the hummingbird!

Colorful, with iridescent feathers, they are so small that they can fly faster than most birds. They are attracted to a variety of colorful plants, leading people to want to grow these types of plants to view them during different times of the year. Small shrubs, tall shrubs, tall plants, and those that even give nectar help attract these colorful birds, leaving you with the ability to view them and watch as they land, feed, and fly. Given the fact that there are a variety of these plants, here are the most recommended **flowering plants that attract hummingbirds**.

## 1. Butterfly Bush

These shrubs are very versatile, and they begin blooming from early spring all the way through the summer and into the fall. They come in many assorted colors including purple, white, pink, red and yellow. They are very fragrant and can grow up to 10 feet tall, attracting not only hummingbirds but also butterflies! That's what makes them one of the best **flowering plants that attract hummingbirds**.

## 2. Salvia

Recognized by their vertical and vibrant flowers, they can be found in blue, red, violet, and even pink. They hold a long bloom period, giving you not only color but also hummingbird attraction throughout the summer.

## 3. Cardinal Flower

This is a perennial flower that grows up to four feet tall, however, it loves to soak up full sun, and is easy to grow. Giving red flowers in the early summer, this plant attracts hummingbirds to its blooms.

Hummingbird territory stretches from the north to the south, with migration areas extending well into South America. Over the years, they have become a favorite for gardeners and bird watchers who appreciate their beautiful feathers. These small birds have a large personality though. They have been known to fight off larger intruders, including hawks. They will absolutely chase other birds from their territory. Nicknamed the “Avian Helicopter”, these versatile creatures live life in the fast lane. Create your own backyard restaurant for these birds and enjoy the view! Always remember, be humble and kind to nature.
