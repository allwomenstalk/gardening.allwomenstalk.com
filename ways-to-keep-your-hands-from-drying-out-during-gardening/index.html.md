# 7 Ways to Keep Your Hands from Drying out during Gardening ...

If you like to garden, be sure you know the best ways to keep your hands from drying out. Gardening is a great way to relieve stress and it can also be a great form of keep active. Yet, all that exposure to the sun, or even chemicals in certain gloves, can all dry your hands out. To keep that from happening, just implement some handy ways to keep your hands from drying out during gardening instead. No more cracking, peeling, or scaly hands! These tips have got you covered all the way!

## 1. Coconut Oil

This oil is said to be good for everything these days, and it’s also one of the best ways to keep your hands from drying out during gardening. Coconut oil is one of the best natural lubricants and it absorbs quickly into the skin, unlike other oils. It’s also chemical free and even has antiviral properties. Many people also apply coconut oil before going into the sun since it acts as a natural protecting agent.

## 2. Soap Smart

When you wash all that dirt off your hands, be careful what kind of soaps you choose. Some with antibacterial ingredients also contain chemicals that dry your hands out. It’s best to use plain old soap, and not the fancy stuff. Use a natural soap like Dr. Bronner’s or a bar form with natural plant oils and no additives. Dove’s soap-free beauty bar is also a great option.

## 3. Chemical-free

When you apply any form of sunscreen to yourself, always choose chemical-free if possible. Some forms can dry out your hands if you’re not careful. It’s best to apply these to your face first, and then rinse, and apply coconut oil afterwards.

## 4. Soil Smart

If you’re purchasing fertilizers and soil products that contain chemicals, these might also dry your hands out. Be sure to wear gloves while applying these so your hands don’t become dry or irritated.

## 5. Water Works

Most people’s tap water is laced with chlorine, which can dry your hands out fast. When washing your hands, be sure to apply coconut oil or your favorite lotion on afterwards to prevent your hands from drying out. It’s best to apply a filter on your tap to prevent this from happening at all.

## 6. Sunburn

If your hands are exposed to the sun too much, they could suffer minor sunburn. Applying SPF is fine, but some can contain chemicals that might dry you out too. It’s best to wear gloves as much as possible to prevent this from happening. You might also find many sunscreen products especially for gardeners at specialty stores as another option.

## 7. Stay Hydrated

Believe it or not, if you’re not hydrating internally, you can suffer dehydration symptoms like dry lips and hands. Be sure to drink plenty of water to keep your whole body hydrated, including your hands. Keep a water bottle handy beside you during the day and make sure you refill it often. Even if you don't feel thirsty, drink something every 30 minutes for optimum hydration.

Gardening is usually a pretty safe hobby, but it's still best to be aware of precautions like this. We sure don't want those lovely hands of yours drying out! This can cause cracking, peeling, and bleeding, which is just no fun! Do you suffer dry hands if you garden? What seems to help?
