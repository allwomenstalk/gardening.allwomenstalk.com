63. Peace Rose

Via Sweet Peace Rose by Kathy ...

Correctly called the Madame A. Meilland, the Peace rose has a special story. The rose was developed in the years 1935-1939 by Frenchman Francis Meilland. When he foresaw war coming he shipped cuttings to friends in various countries around the world so it would be safeguarded. After the war, a Peace rose was presented to each delegation at the inaugural meeting of the United Nations with the message "We hope the 'Peace' rose will influence men’s thoughts for everlasting world peace."

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes a rose unique?
Ah, it's all about the color, shape, fragrance, and even the history behind it! Some roses have such unique petal formations or rare color blends that just make them stand out. Kind of like a rare gem in a sea of flowers.
Why is the 'Martha Washington Rose' so special?
This rose is a classic! It's renowned for its stunning blooms and rich history. It's like a little piece of garden royalty. If you love historical plants with a story, this one’s for you.
What's the 'Mint Julep Rose'? It sounds so refreshing!
Doesn't it? The Mint Julep Rose has this refreshing name and looks to match. Think of it as a rose that brings a cool, crisp vibe to your garden with its unique greenish hues. It's a real conversation starter!
How do I choose the most beautiful rose for my garden?
It's so personal! Some people go for color, others for fragrance, or even the rose’s ability to bloom frequently. My advice? Visit a garden center, see which one catches your eye and strokes your heart. It should feel like love at first sight!
Can anyone really grow these gorgeous roses at home?
Absolutely! With the right care, soil, and a bit of patience, almost anyone can grow beautiful roses. Some might need a bit more TLC, but the effort is always worth it when you see those first blooms. From your garden to your heart!
